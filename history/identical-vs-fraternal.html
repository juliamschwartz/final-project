Many people who are not twins do not know the difference between identical and fraternal and how this distinction occurs.

Identical twins occur when a fertilized egg separtes into two embryos. In this case, each embryo has the same set of genetics and therefore, get the name identical. A mother gives birth to fraternal twins when two eggs instead of the usual one are fertilized and grow. Fraternal twins are always separate, and this is why fraternal twins can be a boy and a girl. However, just because a set of twins are the same gender it does not mean they are indentical.

Now that you know the difference between both types of twins, here are a few facts about identical twins:

  • Identical twins do not have the same fingerprints. While they start off the same, they change during weeks 6-13 of development
  • Mirror twins are identical twins that are exact reflections of each other, hence the name
  • 40% of identical twins create their own language when they are babies to commute with each other(I am part of this percentage)
